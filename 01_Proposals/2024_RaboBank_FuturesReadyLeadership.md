# Proposal: Futures-Ready Leadership Programme
**Client:** Rabobank — Group Strategy & Leadership
**Date:** March 2024
**Status:** Won

## Client
Rabobank is a Dutch cooperative bank with ~43,000 employees globally. The brief came from Group Strategy in partnership with the Global Head of Leadership Development.

## Brief
Rabobank's senior leadership team (top 120 leaders globally) needed to develop a shared understanding of futures thinking and what it means for the bank's strategic positioning. The ask was a high-impact leadership experience — not a training programme, not a strategy offsite, but something in between that would shift how leaders think about uncertainty and long-term relevance.

## Minkowski Read
The real challenge was not about the future of banking. It was about helping senior leaders let go of a planning mindset and adopt a navigating mindset. The bank has strong strategic capabilities but limited tolerance for ambiguity in leadership conversations. The programme needed to make futures thinking feel both credible and personally relevant to a financially conservative leadership culture.

## Proposed Approach
A 2.5-day residential programme structured in three movements:

**Movement 1 — Seeing Further**
Futures literacy fundamentals. Scanning weak signals. Scenario logic. Half-day with all 120 leaders together.

**Movement 2 — Thinking Differently**
Small groups of 15 working through 4 strategic scenarios developed for Rabobank's context. Facilitated dialogue on strategic implications. Full day.

**Movement 3 — Deciding Under Uncertainty**
Cross-group synthesis. Strategic choices exercise. Personal leadership commitments. Half-day closing plenary.

## Team
- Sarah Chen — lead futurist and content design
- Marc de Vries — programme lead and facilitation design
- 2 additional facilitators from Minkowski network for breakout groups

## Format
Residential. Venue: Kasteel de Wittenburg, Wassenaar. 2.5 days.

## Investment
€185,000 programme fee + €28,000 venue and logistics.
Excludes travel for international participants.

## Outcome
Programme delivered May 2024. Strong NPS from participants (47). Follow-on conversation started for 2025 middle management programme.

## Reuse Notes
The three-movement structure (Seeing / Thinking / Deciding) is transferable. The scenario logic works for any large-cap financial services client. The 'planning mindset vs navigating mindset' framing resonated strongly — worth reusing in proposals for similar client cultures.
