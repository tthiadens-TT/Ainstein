# 02_Tools — Agent README

*Consolidated 2026-04-18. Single source of truth for Minkowski's operational method layer. Canonical tool landscape sourced from Minkowski materials; practical facilitation companions marked as seed content and clearly separated.*

## Purpose
This folder contains the reusable working system of Minkowski: the methods, frameworks, exercises, facilitation formats, and operational building blocks used to design and deliver programs.

For the agent, this folder is the operational method layer. It explains **how Minkowski works**, **which tools are explicitly part of the current source material**, and **how they relate to delivery**.

## Important note
The tool landscape below is deliberately limited to tools, methods, frameworks, exercises, and operational elements that are **explicitly mentioned** in the currently available Minkowski sources. It does **not** introduce new tools, inferred methods, or speculative labels.

The "Facilitation companions" section at the end (Futures Readiness Canvas, 2-Day Futures Sprint) is **seed content**: practical how-to material generated to fill retrieval gaps. It is grounded in the canonical tools above but should be adapted before client use.

## Part 1 — Current tool landscape from Minkowski source material

### 1. SCOPE for Change
**Type:** flagship program framework / change model
**Purpose:** Minkowski's core approach to helping organizations turn strategic plans into reality by combining futures thinking with leadership development.
**How it works:** The framework is structured around four movements:
- **Awaken**
- **Assess**
- **Accelerate**
- **Activate**

These movements are translated into a 10-module program that helps create a shared vision of the future, surface limiting beliefs, align current reality to future ambition, and activate behavior change.

**Linked modules:**
1. Creating a level playing field
2. Awaken to the Futures
3. Awaken to the Self
4. Assess your Limits
5. Assess your Beliefs
6. Accelerate to the Present
7. Accelerate your Actions
8. Activate the Future
9. Activate your Futures
10. Paying it forward

**Primary use case:** strategic change, leadership development, team alignment, futures readiness.

### 5. Futures Cone
**Type:** futures thinking tool / sensemaking exercise
**Purpose:** To map trends, observations, possible futures, likely impact, and timing.

**Source description:** In Module 2, participants map trends and observations relevant to the organization "on a futures cone" to understand impact, timing, and likelihood.

**Role in delivery:** One of the clearest explicitly named practical tools in the source material.
**Primary use case:** trend mapping, strategic foresight, exploring changing possibilities, building a shared view of the future.
**Linked SCOPE module:** Module 2 — *Awaken to the Futures*

**Practical facilitation notes (seed — adapt before use):**
The cone separates the future into four widening bands — the **probable**, the **plausible**, the **possible**, and the **preferable**. Used to widen a leadership team's peripheral vision before they commit to a strategy.

- **When to use:** early in a leadership programme before strategy work; when a client is locked into extrapolation; as the opening lens for scenario building; when the brief asks for "innovation" but the real gap is imagination range.
- **How we run it:** (1) anchor the group in one concrete decision or bet they currently hold (the probable); (2) push outward to what is plausible given weak signals; (3) push further to what is possible if one or two system assumptions break; (4) collapse back to the preferable — the future we want to actively shape.
- **Typical duration:** 90 minutes standalone, half a day combined with scenario building.
- **Outputs:** a stretched decision-space map annotated with signals; a short list of "futures we have been ignoring"; a preferable-future statement carried into strategic activation.
- **Common failure modes:** teams stay in the probable because they confuse rigor with narrowness; the preferable becomes a vision statement and loses its decision edge; signals are collected but never linked back to current bets.

## Part 3 — Facilitation companions (seed content)
Practical how-to material grounded in the canonical tools above. Adapt before client use.

### A. Futures Readiness Canvas
A one-page workshop canvas that translates futures thinking into an honest read of organisational readiness. Used as the bridge between a foresight session (Futures Cone / Scenario Building) and strategic activation.

**Format.** One A1 canvas, six boxes. 60–90 minutes. Works with groups of 6–20. One facilitator and one synthesiser.

**The six boxes.**
1. **Signals we cannot ignore** — weak or strong signals that have shifted our assumption base in the past 12 months. List 5–7.
2. **Futures we are implicitly betting on** — the future our current plans, budgets, and hires are assuming. Write the assumption out loud.
3. **Futures we are avoiding** — plausible futures that would make our current strategy look naive. Name them.
4. **Readiness gaps** — where we are structurally unprepared (capability, governance, talent, mindset).
5. **The leadership move** — the one decision leadership needs to take to become meaningfully more ready in the next 6 months.
6. **First concrete step** — what can be started, not announced, within two weeks.

**Facilitation prompts.**
- "Which box is the team avoiding? Go there first."
- "If we found out in six months that box 3 had materialised, what would we wish we had done?"
- "Box 5 should feel uncomfortable. If it does not, it is not the real move."

**Outputs to capture.** Completed canvas (photo + typed); leadership move sentence (box 5, clean); named owner and two-week action (box 6).

**When not to use.** When the group has not yet built a shared view of plausible futures — run a Futures Cone / Scenario Building session first. When the challenge is operational rather than strategic.

### B. 2-Day Futures Sprint
A compressed format that takes a leadership team from scattered concern to a shared, actionable read of their strategic position within 48 hours. Minkowski's workhorse format when a client needs momentum without a multi-month programme.

**Audience.** Executive teams, 8–16 participants. Works best when the CEO or business unit lead is in the room for the full two days.

**Day 1 — Widen.**
| Block | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Opening & framing | 45 min | Why now, rules of engagement, essence statement shared back to the group. |
| Signals wall | 90 min | Participants bring pre-read signals; facilitator clusters live. |
| Futures Cone session | 90 min | From probable to preferable (tool #5 above). |
| Scenario sketching | 2 hrs | Four scenarios, one table each, narrative outputs (tool #6). |
| Dinner reflection | 90 min | Informal but facilitated prompt: "what moved for me today?" |

**Day 2 — Sharpen.**
| Block | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Implications workshop | 90 min | For each scenario: what breaks, what becomes possible. |
| Futures Readiness Canvas | 90 min | Full group completes the canvas (section A above). |
| Leadership move | 60 min | Name the one decision. Pressure-test it. |
| Activation planning | 90 min | Owner, milestones, first two weeks. |
| Close | 30 min | Each participant commits one thing out loud. |

**Team shape.** Lead facilitator (Senior Futurist) + co-facilitator/synthesiser (Facilitator/Coach) + optional subject-matter expert for the implications workshop (half day). See `03_Pricing/worked_example_midmarket_leadership.md` for indicative commercial shape.

**Outputs.** Signals map; four scenario narratives; completed Futures Readiness Canvas; leadership move statement; 14-day activation plan with named owner.

**What makes this format Minkowski.** The sprint ends with a named decision, not a summary deck. Experts enter as challengers, not teachers. The preferable future is explicit, not implied.

**Common adaptations.**
- 1-day compressed: drop scenario sketching, keep cone + canvas + move.
- 3-day expanded: add a client immersion day before day 1.
- Remote: workable but loses ~30% of the group's synthesis quality; not our recommendation.

- Client-specific proposals (see `01_Proposals/`).

Name files by function first, not by creator. Examples:
- `SCOPEforChange_Framework`
- `EDIT_ProgramDesign_Process`
- `FuturesCone_WorkshopCanvas`
- `ScenarioBuilding_FacilitationGuide`

- `ExperimentDesign_ActivationTemplate`

## Agent instructions
When using this folder, the agent should:
1. Distinguish clearly between **framework**, **tool**, **method**, **exercise**, **template**, **facilitation format**, and **delivery role**.
2. Only describe something as a Minkowski tool when it is explicitly evidenced in source material (Part 1 above). Clearly mark Part 3 content as facilitation seed, not canon.
3. Map each item to where it sits in delivery: diagnose / design / facilitate / activate / evaluate / scale.
4. Link tools back to SCOPE modules where relevant.
5. Prefer the most reusable operational version over the most polished or decorative version.
6. Avoid duplication when describing materially the same method.

Prioritise **accuracy, reusability, clarity, and transferability** over style.
