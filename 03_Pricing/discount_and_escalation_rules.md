# Discount and Escalation Rules

*Seed content generated 2026-04-18. Adapt before client use. Supersedes nothing.*

## Principle
Price discipline protects the offer. Every discount is a statement about what Minkowski is worth. We discount deliberately, not defensively.

## Margin floors

| Margin | Meaning | Who approves |
|---|---|---|
| ≥ 40% | Healthy. Proceed. | Partner level. |
| 35–40% | Acceptable. Flag in proposal review. | Partner level. |
| 25–35% | Below standard. Requires a written justification. | Partner + commercial lead. |
| < 25% | Escalate before quoting. | Full partnership decision. |

## When we discount (deliberately)

1. **Strategic logo** — a named account where the reference value is real and demonstrable. Cap: 15% off list. Document the portfolio reason.
2. **Multi-programme commitment** — client signs for 2+ programmes in a 12-month window. Cap: 10% off list on the second and third programme, not the first.
3. **Partner / white-label work** — we deliver through another agency. Net rate agreed upfront, not discounted ad hoc.
4. **Research / development investment** — we want the case study or the method iteration. Treat as R&D, not as discounting.

## When we do not discount

- To match a competitor on price. We compete on fit, not on list price.
- Because the buyer "needs to show procurement a win."
- Because the programme slipped into next year's budget.
- To close a deal that should have been reframed into a clear essence statement first.
- To keep a Senior Futurist utilised — utilisation is a planning problem, not a pricing problem.

## Escalation triggers
Escalate before sending a number when any of these hold:

- Margin below 25%.
- Client requests scope creep labelled as "just a small add."
- Payment terms beyond 60 days net.
- Exclusive rights to method or collateral.
- Rate card being referenced outside a single programme context.
- Free pilot or free diagnostic requested as a condition of pitching.

## When to walk away
Walk is a live option, not a last resort. Walk when:

- The client cannot name the decision the programme serves, and refuses to reframe.
- The buyer cannot resource the participants' time honestly.
- Procurement rules make it impossible to protect faculty quality.
- The work would damage the integrity of the Minkowski offer if delivered badly.

## Recording the decision
Every deliberate discount or below-floor decision is noted in the proposal archive with: reason, approver, expected return. This is how we learn. See `01_Proposals/` for recent examples.
