# Pricing Decision Matrix

*Seed content generated 2026-04-18. Adapt before client use. Supersedes nothing.*

## How to use
Pair this matrix with `programme_pricing_2024.md` (day rates and programme tiers) and `discount_and_escalation_rules.md` (when to flex). This document translates client profile × brief complexity × team shape into an indicative tier.

## Client profile axis

| Profile | Typical signals | Default tier bias |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate, large-cap | >€5B revenue, >50 participants, procurement involved | Mid or large |
| Corporate, mid-market | €500M–€5B, 15–40 participants | Mid |
| Growth / scale-up | Series C+, founder-present, fast decisions | Small or mid |
| Public / semi-public | Ministries, regulators, universities | Small or mid, longer cycle |
| Partner agency (white label) | Reselling our work | Small, reduced margin |

## Brief complexity axis

| Complexity | Markers | Effect on tier |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Single format (e.g. one workshop), one audience, clear outcome | Pull toward Small |
| Medium | Multi-session programme, one expert discipline, one client stakeholder group | Sit in Mid |
| High | Multi-module, multi-expert, board + ExCo + layer below, multi-country | Push toward Large |
| Ambiguous | Client cannot name the decision the programme serves | **Do not price — reframe first** |

## Team shape axis

| Shape | Composition | Cost driver |
|---|---|---|
| Lean | 1 Senior Futurist + 1 Facilitator | Baseline |
| Standard | 1 Senior + 1 Facilitator + 1 SME | +15–25% |
| Full bench | 2 Seniors + 2 Facilitators + 2–3 SMEs | +40–60% |

## Quick lookup (indicative, not binding)

| Client × Complexity × Shape | Indicative tier | Indicative range |
|---|---|---|
| Large-cap × High × Full bench | Large | €150–200k |
| Large-cap × Medium × Standard | Mid | €60–90k |
| Mid-market × Medium × Lean | Mid (low) | €45–60k |
| Growth × Low × Lean | Small | €18–28k |
| Public × Medium × Standard | Mid (low) | €45–65k |
| Partner agency × Medium × Lean | Small | €20–30k net |

## Before you send a number
1. Has the brief been sharpened into a clear essence statement? If not, stop.
2. Is the buyer in the room who signs? If not, flag the risk.
3. Does the shape match the brief, or are we over-resourcing to look serious? Trim.
4. Does margin clear 35%? If not, see `discount_and_escalation_rules.md`.

## What this matrix does not replace
- Judgment about strategic value of the client logo.
- Negotiation posture in long-term partnerships.
- The explicit choice to invest below margin for portfolio reasons.
