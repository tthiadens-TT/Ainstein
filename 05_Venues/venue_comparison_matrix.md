# Venue Comparison Matrix

*Seed content generated 2026-04-18. Adapt before client use. Supersedes nothing.*

A practical lookup for matching programme type to venue archetype. Use after the programme shape is clear — not as the opening move.

## Matrix

| Programme type | Duration | Best-fit venue archetype | Avoid | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1-day Futures Sprint | 1 day | Boutique creative (Amsterdam NDSM type) | Hotel boardroom | The day is about widening; the venue should do part of the work. |
| 2-day Futures Sprint, local | 2 days | Canal-side townhouse **or** boutique creative | Conference hotel centre | Sprint needs coherence; too many rooms dilute focus. |
| 2–3 day leadership offsite, regional | 2–3 days | Lisbon/Porto coastal retreat | City-centre hotel | Distance + residential setting multiply focus. |
| Multi-module corporate programme | 3–6 modules over 2–6 months | Conference hotel (Zuidas) for most modules, boutique for opening | Rotating venues per module | Participants should recognise the room. |
| Executive intensive, 8–12 leaders | 2–4 days | Canal-side townhouse or Lisbon villa | Any large conference space | Intimacy is the product. |
| Client-facing showcase | Half to full day | Museum/cultural venue or members' club | Own office | Signal value matters. |
| Innovation / transformation lab | 2–3 days | Berlin industrial space | London members' club | The room contradicts the message otherwise. |
| Board-level futures session | Half to 1 day | London members' club **or** canal-side townhouse | Industrial creative space | Credibility signals matter more than disruption signals. |

## Trade-off rules of thumb

1. **Budget vs. signal.** A venue that signals "different thinking" is usually worth ~10–15% more than a neutral venue for formats designed to stretch perspective.
2. **Distance vs. attrition.** Every hour of extra travel costs ~5–10% of full attendance at the margins. For senior groups, attendance matters more than aesthetic.
3. **Residential vs. commuter.** Residential programmes compound insight; commuter programmes preserve work continuity. Match to the programme's goal, not to logistics convenience.
4. **Familiar vs. unfamiliar.** A familiar venue reduces friction but also reduces the "something is different here" signal. For breakthrough work, choose unfamiliar.

## What we do not do
- We do not host core Minkowski programmes at client offices. Exceptions require a written reason.
- We do not rotate venues across modules in one programme.
- We do not book high-signal venues (museums, members' clubs) for working sessions whose content is mundane — it creates expectation mismatch.
