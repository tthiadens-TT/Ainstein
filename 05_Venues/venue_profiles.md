## Amsterdam

### 1. Boutique creative venue (NDSM / North)
- **Archetype:** Industrial creative space, 30–60 capacity.
- **Best for:** Futures sprints, leadership offsites where the physical setting should signal "this is not business as usual."
- **Strengths:** Natural light, large wall surfaces for signals walls and canvases, strong café/food culture around it, ferry-in arrival ritual.
- **Watch-outs:** Weather-dependent arrival; less suitable for executive teams that prize discretion; limited breakout rooms.
- **Typical cost shape:** €2,500–€4,500/day including basic catering.

### 2. Conference-class hotel (Zuidas / centre)
- **Archetype:** 4–5 star hotel with dedicated meeting floors, 20–200 capacity.
- **Best for:** Multi-day programmes with international participants, modules that need simultaneous breakouts, residential format.
- **Strengths:** Predictable, well-staffed, rooms on-site, procurement-friendly.
- **Watch-outs:** Visually generic — requires intentional design work to avoid the "any consultancy" feeling; expensive per head.
- **Typical cost shape:** €180–€320 per delegate/day + rooms.

### 3. Canal-side townhouse (centre)
- **Archetype:** Private townhouse, 12–25 capacity.
- **Best for:** Executive-team intimate sessions, board-level futures work, senior leadership coaching intensives.
- **Strengths:** Signals care and craft; discreet; easy to host dinner on-site.
- **Watch-outs:** Limits group size hard; noise constraints; accessibility varies.
- **Typical cost shape:** €3,000–€5,500/day, often with minimum F&B spend.

### 4. Museum / cultural venue (varies)
- **Archetype:** Partnered access to a museum meeting space or gallery room.
- **Best for:** Closing events, "bringing the future to life" sessions, client-facing showcases.
- **Strengths:** High signal value; memorable; often pairs with an exhibit relevant to the theme.
- **Watch-outs:** Booking lead time long; AV often adequate but not great; less flexible for deep-work formats.
- **Typical cost shape:** €4,000–€8,000 for the space; add catering.

## European offsites

### 5. Berlin — repurposed industrial space
- **Archetype:** Converted factory or power station, 40–120 capacity.
- **Best for:** Innovation and transformation programmes, multi-national groups where Berlin signals "different rules."
- **Strengths:** Raw aesthetic counters polished corporate language; strong food and hospitality ecosystem; participants arrive primed for "we are doing something unusual."
- **Watch-outs:** Travel logistics from Southern Europe add a half-day; AV can be variable in older buildings.
- **Typical cost shape:** €3,500–€7,000/day for the space; add catering and rooms.

### 6. London — private members' club or classic conference hotel
- **Archetype:** Central London members' club or traditional hotel with boardroom feel, 15–50 capacity.
- **Best for:** Senior leadership sessions where credibility and discretion matter more than disruption, financial services and legal clients.
- **Strengths:** Signals seriousness; strong service culture; proximity to participants' offices reduces attrition.
- **Watch-outs:** Day rates high; the setting reinforces the status quo — we compensate through strong facilitation rather than venue provocation.
- **Typical cost shape:** £4,000–£9,000/day including basic F&B.

### 7. Lisbon / Porto — coastal retreat setting
- **Archetype:** Boutique hotel or villa, coastal or hillside, 20–40 capacity.
- **Best for:** 3–5 day residential programmes, executive leadership intensives, regeneration-themed work.
- **Strengths:** Distance creates focus; climate allows outdoor sessions most of the year; strong price-to-quality ratio versus Northern Europe.
- **Watch-outs:** Travel time from Northern Europe is half a day each way; less suitable for programmes that need ad-hoc client office drop-ins.
- **Typical cost shape:** €250–€450 per delegate/day fully loaded (rooms + meals + space).

## Selection quick rules
- If the programme needs to change how people think, use a venue that changes how they arrive.
- If the programme is residential, choose boring over distracting — the content should carry the room.
- Never host a "becoming future-ready" session in a space designed for quarterly reviews.
- The further the venue, the longer the programme needs to justify it. Do not fly people for a 4-hour session.
- Choose geography that matches the programme's message. Regeneration theme and glass tower do not pair.
- Check participant mix for caregiving and accessibility constraints before defaulting to remote-coastal options.
