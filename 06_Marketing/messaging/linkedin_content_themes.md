# LinkedIn Content Themes

*Seed content generated 2026-04-18. Adapt before client use. Supersedes nothing.*

LinkedIn is where most of our buyers see us between conversations. The goal of our posting is not reach — it is to stay recognisable to 800 senior people who might buy from us in the next 18 months.

## Posting principles
- **Signal, not volume.** One strong post per fortnight beats three reactive ones per week.
- **Write from the room, not from the deck.** Stories about how leadership decisions actually move are stronger than frameworks in slide form.
- **Name the discomfort.** If a post does not risk disagreement from at least one thoughtful reader, it is too safe.
- **No inspiration porn.** We do not post quotes over sunset photos.

## Four themes we rotate

### Theme A — Inside the Room
Short reflections from live programmes (anonymised). What shifted, what resisted, what surprised us. These build trust that we actually do the work.

### Theme B — Weak Signal of the Week
One observation from the futures practice — a data point, a shift, a contradiction — plus one question we are sitting with. Builds authority on the futures discipline without lecturing.

### Theme C — The Reframe
A client question we deliberately did not answer directly, and why. Demonstrates how we challenge briefs. Works well for leadership buyers who are tired of agencies that say yes to everything.

### Theme D — Craft notes
Short pieces on programme design — why we chose a venue, why a module runs 90 minutes not 60, why the preferable future sentence matters. Distinctive because most agencies do not share this.

## Ten example hooks

1. "The brief said 'leadership development.' The actual decision was 'should we merge these two business units.' We nearly missed it."
2. "The most useful thing we did with a ExCo last quarter was cancel the afternoon."
3. "Scenario planning fails when no-one in the room owns a consequence. Here's what we changed."
4. "We stopped using the word 'future-proof' three years ago. Here's why."
5. "One question that resets a vague leadership brief in under ten minutes."
6. "Why we host almost no programmes at client offices — and what changed when we stopped."
7. "The signal we keep pointing at that most European boards are still underweighting."
8. "A good expert in the room adds friction, not content. Here's the difference."
9. "The part of our pricing conversation we used to skip, and what it cost us."
10. "The moment in a futures sprint where the group stops performing and starts thinking."

## What we avoid
- Carousel posts about "the 7 rules of" anything.
- Hot takes on stories outside our practice.
- Posts that could be signed by any consultancy.
- Announcements without substance (logos, awards, panels) unless tied to a specific insight.

## Publishing cadence
- One Theme A or B post every 10–14 days.
- One Theme C or D post per month.
- Partner and faculty reshare with commentary is worth more than a new post most weeks.
