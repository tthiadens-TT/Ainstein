# Message Pillars

*Seed content generated 2026-04-18. Adapt before client use. Supersedes nothing.*

Four pillars that carry the Minkowski story across proposals, pitches, LinkedIn, website, and client conversations. Each pillar: the claim, the proof, the watch-out.

## Pillar 1 — Applied futures, not speculation

**Claim.** Futures thinking only matters when it changes a decision. Minkowski exists to make that translation happen.

**Proof.** Every programme ends with a named leadership decision, an owner, and a 14-day next step. We publish the format, not the predictions.

**Watch-out.** Do not let this pillar slide into "we are practical consultants." The futures discipline is real; it just does not stop at the scenario.

## Pillar 2 — Faculty as challenge, not content

**Claim.** The experts we bring are there to sharpen the client's thinking, not to teach generic frameworks.

**Proof.** Our faculty profiles lead with the friction they create in the room, not with titles. Our decision layer matches experts to the specific challenge, not to the calendar.

**Watch-out.** Do not let this pillar overclaim — not every session needs an external expert. Sometimes the Minkowski team carries the room.

## Pillar 3 — Programmes designed as experiences

**Claim.** How leadership teams arrive, what they see, and how they move through a programme does more work than any slide deck.

**Proof.** We design room, sequence, and ritual with the same care as the content. Venues are chosen to signal the work, not to be convenient.

**Watch-out.** Experience design without substance is aesthetic. Every design choice should serve the decision the programme is built around.

## Pillar 4 — Commercial honesty

**Claim.** We are explicit about what we will do, what we will not do, what it costs, and when to walk away.

**Proof.** Our proposals show scope, assumptions, and boundaries. Our pricing has a margin floor. We reframe vague briefs before we quote.

**Watch-out.** Commercial honesty is not bluntness. Say hard things with care, not with edge.

## How to use these pillars
- One pillar per piece of external content. Do not stack.
- Proposals can reference two pillars when the brief warrants (usually 1 and 3 or 1 and 4).
- When in doubt, lead with Pillar 1. It is the distinctiveness.

## What not to do with the pillars
- Turn them into a taxonomy and then ignore them in proposals.
- Use them as section headers in client-facing documents. They are narrative, not structure.
- Dilute them to make them easier to sell. The specificity is the point.
