# Core Positioning

*Seed content generated 2026-04-18. Adapt before client use. Supersedes nothing.*

## One-line positioning
**Minkowski is the agency for applied futures — we help leadership teams become future-ready through futures thinking, credible expertise, and carefully designed learning experiences that lead to decisions, not decks.**

## What this means, unpacked
- **Applied, not speculative.** We do not sell foresight as an intellectual product. We sell readiness — decisions and movement inside the client organisation.
- **Agency, not consultancy.** We design and facilitate experiences; we do not write strategy reports for signing.
- **Leadership teams, not everyone.** Our primary audience is executive teams and the layer below. We are not a broad corporate learning provider.

## Who we are for
- Executive teams facing a multi-year strategic horizon that their current planning rhythm cannot handle.
- Leadership development heads who have exhausted generic "future of leadership" content.
- CEOs and business unit leads who need their top team to share a future, not just a Gantt chart.

## Who we are not for
- Organisations looking for a motivational speaker.
- Teams that want certainty, not clarity.
- Procurement-led processes where the lowest price wins.
- Clients who cannot name the decision the programme serves.

## Value pillars
Four pillars carry the narrative across proposals, pitches, and client conversations. They are defined in detail — with claims, proof points, and watch-outs — in `../messaging/message_pillars.md`. Titles only:

1. Applied futures, not speculation.
2. Faculty as challenge, not content.
3. Programmes designed as experiences.
4. Commercial honesty.

Always use the full version in `message_pillars.md` when writing external content; the summary above is only a reminder that these four exist.

## Proof signals we use
- Named programmes with measurable leadership decisions downstream.
- Faculty credibility — senior practitioners, not lecturers.
- Consistent design language across proposals, rooms, and follow-through.

## What we avoid saying
- "Future-proof." (Overclaim.)
- "Thought leadership." (Empty category.)
- "End-to-end transformation." (Not our frame.)
- "Disruptive." (Lazy.)

## How this positioning shows up
- In proposals: we open with the decision, not with our methodology.
- In conversations: we push back on vague briefs before quoting.
- In rooms: we prioritise insight over airtime.
