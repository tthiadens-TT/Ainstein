# One-Pager Template

*Seed content generated 2026-04-18. Adapt before client use. Supersedes nothing.*

A single-page document used early in a conversation — after a discovery call, before a proposal. Goal: give the buyer something they can forward internally that holds our positioning in under 300 words.

## Structure

```
[MINKOWSKI LOGO]

For: [Client name and buyer role]
Subject: [Programme working title — usually the essence statement]
Date: [Date]

---

## The decision this is built around
[One sentence. The essence statement. No hedging.]

## Why now
[Two to three sentences. What has changed in the client's environment
or internal context that makes this the right moment. Use one external
signal and one internal signal.]

## What we propose
[Three to five bullets. Each one is a module or workstream. Each bullet
is written as an outcome, not an activity.
e.g. "A shared read of the four scenarios that matter for your 2027
strategy, built with your top 30 leaders over two days."]

## Who runs it
[Lead Senior Futurist + Facilitator/Coach + any named expert, with a
one-line credibility signal each. Avoid titles-only.]

## Indicative commercial shape
[One line. Tier, range, what it includes, what is billed separately.
e.g. "Mid-tier programme, €55–65k, inclusive of design, delivery, and
closing memo. Venue and catering billed at cost."]

## What happens next
[Two or three concrete steps with dates. Do not end with "let us know
your thoughts."]

---

Minkowski — agency for applied futures
[Contact line]
```

## Writing rules
- Under 300 words total.
- Single page, portrait, comfortable margins.
- One bold sentence at the top — the decision.
- No methodology explainer. We earned the right to that by being in the conversation.
- No generic "about us" paragraph. The positioning is in the specificity.

## What to swap per client
- The "Why now" paragraph — always sector-specific, always refers to at least one client-named priority.
- The "Who runs it" names — match to `04_Experts/` based on brief.
- The "What happens next" dates — never generic. Always named days or weeks.

## What not to change
- The "decision this is built around" framing.
- The positioning line at the bottom.
- The commercial shape format — it prevents ambiguity later.

## When to use
- Within 48 hours of a discovery call that went well.
- When the buyer needs something to forward internally before a full proposal.
- When procurement asks for a summary.

## When not to use
- Before we understand the decision well enough to write the top sentence.
- As a substitute for a proposal.
- For cold outreach — this is a warm document.
